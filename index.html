Sukwon Yun

M.S. in Industrial & Systems Engineering

I am a researcher at Data Science and Artificial Intelligence Lab (DSAIL) at KAIST, where I was advised by Prof. Chanyoung Park during my Master’s. Currently, I am also closely working with Dr. Tianlong Chen at CSAIL@MIT.

During my Master’s year, I had a memorable experience in Tokyo as a visiting researcher at the Murata Lab in Tokyo Tech, under the guidance of Prof. Tsoyoshi Murata, and actively collaborated with with Dr. Xin Liu.

In a nutshell, my primary research interest falls under the keyword AI4Science: Graph Neural Networks with their applications in biomedical domain. I am interested in addressing the inductive bias of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) with their application in the field of Medical and Computational Biology, particularly single-cell RNA sequencing and Tumor Biology. Keywords include but are not limited to the following:

  • Long-Tail Problem on Graphs
  • Missing Features on Graphs
  • Heterophily on Graphs
  • Data Sparsity (Dropout) on scRNA-seq data
  • Tissue Phenotype
